# Why This Is Track 3

Track 3 asks for workflows where specifications evaluate AI-written software. This package implements that pattern directly.

## Multiple Candidates

V3 constructs provider-backed candidate banks across a frozen primary core and expansion suite. It does not evaluate a single final answer in isolation.

## Spec-Driven Discrimination

Selectors operate over fixed banks and compare visible evidence, structural evidence, and spec-aware decisions. Claim B is intentionally support-conditioned: ranking is evaluated only against what the candidate bank actually contains.

## Semantic Regression Catching

Hidden executable evaluators expose visible-pass / hidden-fail cases across the confirmatory suite. This is the main reason the project needs a trust-boundary layer at all.

## Secure Rejection

The strongest positive surface is no-ship behavior. Tests-only selectors falsely accept secure failures much more often than the spec-aware path.

## Acceptance Governance

The system emits `auto_accept`, `auto_reject`, or `escalate_to_review` for selected final artifacts. That makes it an acceptance workflow, not merely a score.

## High-Assurance Trajectory

The denominator is intentionally narrow-waist and security-relevant. The project does not claim full formal assurance, but it identifies where executable specs, support generation, and human review boundaries must meet.
